Why I chose programming:

Funny story - my very first interest in programming started by working on a very small project... for my wife's business class in late 2017. She isn't a huge techie person, so she asked me to help. The project she had to do for her class was to build a resume, but in an HTML format. She was frustrated and didn't understand anything that was happening, so with my tech background and love for [anything] technology, I jumped in and assisted her in making the HTML document. Without any prior knowledge in coding, I managed to finish a VERY basic HTML document that had her basic resume information on it in ~3 hours. This led me to further study and eventually love the IT industry as a whole. It's not just programming and development that excites me about the future; it's the unknown and everything that still needs to be explored. The interesting part about programming is that you always need to continuously learn and keep up with the technology, which I find gratifying and accommodating. It's those that WANT to get better WILL get better, and I KNOW I have what it takes to keep moving forward.
